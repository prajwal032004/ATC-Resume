Expert Resume Building Tips

1. Tailor Your Resume

Customize your resume for each job application. Study the job description and include relevant keywords (e.g., "project management" or "Python") that match the role. This increases your chances of passing Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) used by employers.

2. Highlight Achievements

Instead of listing duties, focus on accomplishments. Use quantifiable metrics like "Increased sales by 20%" or "Managed a team of 5" to show impact. This makes your resume stand out to hiring managers.

3. Keep It Concise

Aim for a one-page resume unless you have extensive experience (10+ years). Use clear, concise language and avoid unnecessary details. Recruiters spend about 6 seconds scanning a resumeâ€”make every word count.

4. Use a Clean Format

Opt for a professional layout with consistent fonts (e.g., Arial, 11-12pt) and plenty of white space. Avoid graphics or colors that might confuse ATS systems. Simple headings like "Education" and "Experience" work best.

5. Include Key Sections

Structure your resume with essential sections: Contact Info, Education, Work Experience, Projects, and Skills. Optional sections like Certifications or Activities can add value if relevant to the job.

6. Proofread Thoroughly

Spelling or grammar errors can ruin your chances. Use tools like Grammarly or ask a friend to review your resume. A polished document shows attention to detailâ€”a trait employers value.
