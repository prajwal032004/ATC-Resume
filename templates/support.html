How to Use ATC Resume

Step 1: Register and Log In

To start building your resume, you need an account:

  1. Go to the homepage and click "Register" in the navbar (or visit /register).
  2. Enter your email, password, and full name, then submit.
  3. After registering, go to /login, enter your credentials, and click "Log In."

Step 2: Build Your Resume

Create your resume with our easy-to-use tool:

  1. Once logged in, navigate to /build-resume from the homepage or navbar.
  2. Fill out the form with your personal info (name, email, phone, location).
  3. Add sections like Education, Work Experience, Projects, and Skills. Use the "Add More" buttons to include multiple entries.
  4. For each section, provide details like dates, descriptions, and bullet points for achievements.
  5. Click "Preview" to see your resume.

Step 3: Preview and Edit

Review and refine your resume:

  1. After submitting the form, you’ll see a preview of your resume.
  2. Check for accuracy and formatting. If needed, go back to edit any section.
  3. Ensure your content is ATS-friendly by using job-specific keywords and avoiding complex layouts.

Step 4: Download Your Resume

Get your resume as a PDF:

  1. From the preview page, click "Download."
  2. The system will generate a PDF file named with your name (e.g., "John_Doe_Resume.pdf").
  3. Save the file to your device and use it for job applications.

Troubleshooting Tips

Can’t Log In

Double-check your email and password. If you’ve forgotten your password, this feature is coming soon—meanwhile, create a new account with a different email.

PDF Download Fails

Ensure all required fields (e.g., name, education) are filled. Try refreshing the page or using a different browser (Chrome or Firefox recommended).

Form Data Not Saving

You must be logged in to save data. If logged in and still facing issues, clear your browser cache or log out and back in.

Preview Looks Wrong

Check for typos or incomplete fields. Avoid special characters or excessive text in descriptions, as they may disrupt formatting.
